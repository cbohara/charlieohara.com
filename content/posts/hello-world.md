I've been a data engineer since 2017. That's a lot of pipelines, a lot of 2 a.m. pages, and a lot of "it works on my machine" moments that did not, in fact, work on the machine that mattered.

For years the lessons lived in my head, in Slack threads, and in commit messages no one will ever read again. This blog is my attempt to write a few of them down — partly to share, mostly because writing forces me to actually understand the thing I thought I understood.

## What I'll write about

- **Data engineering** — pipelines, orchestration, and the unglamorous plumbing that keeps everything flowing.
- **MLOps** — getting models out of notebooks and into production without losing sleep.
- **The mistakes** — the ones I learned the most from, told honestly.

No fixed schedule. I'd rather publish something I mean than fill a content calendar.

## Why now

The simplest reason: I rebuilt this site, and an empty Writing section felt like a dare. So consider this the first post — the one that exists mostly to prove the next one can.

More soon.
